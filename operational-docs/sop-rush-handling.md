# Rush Hour Procedures — Standard Operating Procedure

## Overview
Rush hours are predictable high-volume periods that require proactive preparation, clear communication, and structured execution. This SOP covers the preparation, execution, and wind-down phases of a rush period.

## Rush Hour Definitions

| Rush Period | Weekday Times | Weekend Times | Typical Duration |
|-------------|--------------|---------------|-----------------|
| Morning Rush | 7:00–10:00am | 8:00–11:00am | 2–3 hours |
| Lunch Rush | 11:30am–1:30pm | 11:00am–1:00pm | 1.5–2 hours |
| Afternoon Rush | 2:30–4:00pm | 1:00–3:00pm | 1–1.5 hours |

## Phase 1: Preparation (30 minutes before rush)

### Station Readiness Checklist

**Hot Bar:**
- [ ] Espresso machine warmed up and calibrated (pull test shots)
- [ ] Milk pitchers clean and staged (at least 4 pitchers)
- [ ] All milk alternatives stocked in reach-in (whole, 2%, oat, almond, coconut)
- [ ] Cup sleeves restocked for all sizes
- [ ] Syrup pumps checked and bottles at least half full
- [ ] Backup espresso beans within arm's reach

**Cold Bar:**
- [ ] Ice bins full (request refill from back of house if below 75%)
- [ ] Cold brew kegs connected and flowing (test pour)
- [ ] Blender clean and operational (if applicable)
- [ ] Cold cups and dome lids restocked for all sizes
- [ ] Whipped cream canisters charged (minimum 2 ready)
- [ ] Refresher bases stocked

**Food Station:**
- [ ] Warming oven preheated
- [ ] Pastry case fully stocked and merchandise presentable
- [ ] Sandwich ingredients prepped and portioned
- [ ] Food packaging (bags, wrappers, napkins) stocked
- [ ] Food display labels current and accurate

**Register/POS:**
- [ ] Register drawer balanced and stocked with change
- [ ] Receipt paper full
- [ ] Mobile order printer working and paper loaded
- [ ] Tip jar emptied

### Supply Pre-Staging

Pull the following from back of house and stage within arm's reach of each station:
- 1 sleeve of each cup size (small, medium, large) per bar
- 2 gallons of whole milk, 1 gallon each alternative
- 1 bag of ice (emergency backup)
- 1 package of each pastry type selling above 10 units/day

## Phase 2: Pre-Rush Briefing (15 minutes before rush)

The shift supervisor conducts a 2-minute standing huddle with all floor staff:

### Briefing Template
1. **Volume expectation:** "Today we're expecting [X] orders this hour based on our target and yesterday's actuals."
2. **Order mix forecast:** "Mobile orders are [trending up/stable/down]. Expect [hot/cold] to dominate."
3. **Staffing positions:** Confirm who is on which station. Announce any changes from the posted schedule.
4. **Focus point:** One specific thing to watch for (e.g., "Cold bar was our bottleneck yesterday — let's batch prep iced lattes early today.")
5. **Break schedule:** Confirm when breaks will happen (never during peak 30 minutes).

## Phase 3: Rush Execution

### Communication Protocol

During rush, communication must be **brief, loud, and specific:**

| Situation | What to Say | Who Says It |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Mobile order batch incoming | "Mobile surge — [X] orders dropping in [Y] minutes" | Register/POS person |
| Station falling behind | "Cold bar backing up — [X] in queue" | Person on that station |
| Need help | "Request backup on [station]" | Person on that station |
| Staff reallocation | "[Name], moving you to [station] for the next [X] minutes" | Shift supervisor |
| Running low on supplies | "[Item] running low at [station]" | Person on that station |
| Customer issue | "Customer assist at [location]" | Anyone who notices |

### Order Prioritization During Rush

When the queue is deep, prioritize in this order:
1. **Drive-thru orders** (if applicable) — customers are timed and visible
2. **Mobile orders past their pickup time** — customer is likely already waiting
3. **In-store orders in queue order** — first in, first out
4. **Mobile orders approaching pickup time** — stage for on-time pickup

### Real-Time Monitoring

The shift supervisor (or designated person) should check the following every 10 minutes during rush:

| Metric | Target | Action if Off-Target |
|--------|--------|---------------------|
| Average wait time | < 3 minutes | Reallocate staff from lowest-utilization station |
| Station capacity | All < 100% | See Cold Bar Surge Playbook if any station exceeds 100% |
| Order pace vs target | > 90% of hourly target | Investigate if significantly below (staffing issue? equipment issue?) |
| Mobile order backlog | < 5 orders past pickup time | Prioritize overdue mobile orders |

## Phase 4: Rush Wind-Down (when volume drops below 80% of peak)

### Wind-Down Checklist
1. Return any reallocated staff to their original stations
2. Process pending breaks that were deferred during rush
3. Restock all stations (cups, lids, milk, ice, syrups, food)
4. Clean and sanitize work surfaces (mandatory between rushes)
5. Empty trash and recycling if more than 75% full
6. Restock customer-facing areas (sugar, napkins, stirrers, condiment bar)

### Post-Rush Debrief (optional but recommended)

Quick 1-minute check-in with the team:
- "Peak wait time hit [X] minutes — [above/below] our target"
- "What worked well?"
- "Anything we should do differently for the next rush?"
- Note any equipment issues or supply shortages to address before next rush

## Escalation Triggers

Contact the shift supervisor immediately if any of the following occur during rush:

| Trigger | Why |
|---------|-----|
| Wait time exceeds 6 minutes | Exceeds customer satisfaction threshold |
| Any station at 0 staff (unplanned) | Critical coverage gap |
| Equipment failure (espresso machine, blender, POS) | Requires immediate workaround or repair |
| Customer complaint about wait time | Supervisor should handle directly |
| Supply stockout of a key item (cups, lids, milk) | Supervisor coordinates emergency restock |
| Staff illness or injury during shift | Supervisor arranges coverage and first aid |

## Emergency Rush Protocol

For unexpected high-volume events (200%+ normal volume):
1. Shift supervisor takes over as floor coordinator (no station assignment)
2. Simplify menu if authorized: suspend blended drinks and complex customizations
3. Deploy all available staff to stations (including those on cleaning or stocking duties)
4. Contact on-call staff for immediate assistance
5. Notify store manager via phone/text with situation update
