# Staffing Guidelines — Standard Operating Procedure

## Overview
This document defines minimum staffing levels by station and daypart, rules for staff reallocation, and guidelines for break scheduling. Proper staffing is the single biggest factor in maintaining service quality and wait times.

## Station Definitions

| Station | Role | Typical Throughput (per person per hour) |
|---------|------|----------------------------------------|
| **Hot Bar** | Espresso drinks, hot teas, hot chocolate | 25–30 drinks/hour/person |
| **Cold Bar** | Iced drinks, cold brew, blended drinks, refreshers | 22–28 drinks/hour/person |
| **Food Station** | Warming food items, sandwiches, pastries | 20–25 items/hour/person |
| **Register/POS** | Order taking, payment, customer greeting | 40–50 transactions/hour/person |
| **Drive-Thru** (if applicable) | Window service, order confirmation | 30–35 transactions/hour/person |

## Reallocation Rules

### When to Reallocate

Staff should be moved between stations when:
1. **Any station exceeds 100% capacity** for more than 5 consecutive minutes
2. **Wait times at any station exceed 4 minutes** while another station is below 60% utilization
3. **A surge is detected** (see Cold Bar Surge Playbook or Mobile Order Surge Playbook)
4. **Order mix shifts dramatically** (e.g., cold drinks jump from 40% to 65% of total mix)

### How to Decide Who to Move

Priority order for selecting who to reallocate:
1. **Cross-trained staff** — move someone who is trained on the destination station
2. **Lowest-utilization station** — pull from the station with the most spare capacity
3. **Longest remaining shift** — prefer moving someone with more hours left
4. **Least disruption** — don't move someone mid-order or mid-task

### Reallocation Constraints (NEVER violate)

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Minimum 1 person per active station** | Never leave an active station unstaffed. A station is "active" if it has received an order in the last 15 minutes. |
| **Food station protected during meal hours** | Food station must have at least 1 person during 11am–2pm and 5pm–7pm. |
| **Register always staffed** | At least 1 person on register/POS during all open hours. |
| **No double moves** | Don't move the same person twice within 30 minutes — it's disorienting and reduces productivity. |
| **Communicate before moving** | Always tell the person being moved: where they're going, why, and how long (estimated). |

### Expected Impact of Reallocation

| Action | Typical Impact |
|--------|---------------|
| Move 1 person to overloaded station | Reduces wait time by 30–40% within 10 minutes |
| Move 1 person during surge | Absorbs approximately 25 additional orders/hour |
| Return person after surge | Allow 5 minutes to resettle before measuring impact |

## Break Scheduling

### Rules
- All staff working 6+ hours are entitled to one 30-minute unpaid break and one 15-minute paid break
- All staff working 4–6 hours are entitled to one 15-minute paid break
- **Never schedule two breaks simultaneously** on the same station
- **Avoid breaks during peak hours** (7–10am weekdays, 8–11am weekends, 11am–1pm daily)
- If a break must occur during peak hours, ensure coverage is arranged before the person leaves

### Break Priority
1. Schedule breaks during the lowest-volume hour of each person's shift
2. Stagger breaks at least 30 minutes apart
3. The shift supervisor's break should be scheduled when a senior barista can cover oversight

## Staffing Escalation

### When to Call for Additional Staff

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| All stations above 90% capacity for 15+ minutes | Call shift supervisor to approve overtime or call-in |
| Wait times exceed 6 minutes across the store | Shift supervisor escalates to store manager |
| A station has been at 0 staff for any reason (break, no-show) | Immediately reassign from lowest-utilization station and contact on-call staff |
| Unexpected event (nearby building fire alarm, sports event ending) | Shift supervisor activates emergency staffing protocol |

### On-Call Staff
- Each shift should have 1–2 on-call staff members who can arrive within 30 minutes
- On-call list is maintained in the weekly schedule posted in the back office
- On-call staff should be contacted via the team messaging app first, phone call if no response within 5 minutes
